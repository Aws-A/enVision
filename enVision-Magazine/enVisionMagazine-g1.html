Amazon: Artery of a Continent

The Amazon River, often called the “artery of a continent,” is the lifeblood of South America. Spanning over 6,400 kilometers, it supports the largest rainforest on Earth, housing unparalleled biodiversity.

The river and its tributaries provide water, transportation, and livelihoods for millions. It plays a critical role in global climate regulation by storing vast amounts of carbon.

Indigenous communities have thrived along its banks for centuries, maintaining a deep connection to the ecosystem. However, deforestation and climate change threaten its health. Protecting the Amazon is vital for environmental balance and preserving one of the planet’s most important natural resources.
