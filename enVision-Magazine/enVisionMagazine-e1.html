People Effect on Psychology and Decisions

In 17th-century Netherlands, tulips became a symbol of wealth and beauty. Prices soared as rare tulip bulbs were traded like stocks. At its peak, a single bulb could buy a house.

But in 1637, the bubble burst—buyers vanished, and prices collapsed overnight. The Tulip Mania became one of history’s first recorded financial bubbles.

It’s a cautionary tale of speculation, herd behavior, and irrational markets, reminding us how beauty and hype can lead to ruin.
