Cold War Impact in Modern Politics

The Cold War, lasting from 1947 to 1991, continues to shape modern politics. It fostered a bipolar world dominated by the United States and the Soviet Union, leading to proxy wars, espionage, and an arms race.

The ideological clash between capitalism and communism influenced foreign policy, military alliances, and global economic systems. After the USSR's collapse, NATO expanded, sparking new tensions. Cybersecurity, misinformation campaigns, and geopolitical rivalries today echo Cold War dynamics.
